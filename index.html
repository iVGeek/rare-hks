About

The Artist

I grew up watching Saturday morning anime with a crochet hook in my hand — literally. My grandmother taught me the craft when I was seven, and I've been stitching ever since. But somewhere between Sailor Moon and Samurai Champloo, I realized I could blend my two worlds.

Every YARNVICE cap starts as a sketch inspired by anime frames, hip-hop album covers, or street photography from Tokyo at midnight. I select each yarn by hand, mixing textures and colors the way a DJ mixes tracks — layering, sampling, creating something that shouldn't work but absolutely does.

This isn't fast fashion. It's slow, intentional, handmade art for people who see style as self-expression. Each cap takes 8 to 16 hours to complete, and no two are exactly alike — just like the people who wear them.

Process

The Making

Every cap is a journey from concept to creation. Here's how each piece comes to life.

01

Step 01

Concept & Inspiration

It starts with a frame — a scene from Akira, a graffitied wall in Harajuku, a lyric from a Kendrick track. I sketch ideas and build mood boards around a single emotion.

02

Step 02

Design & Sketching

I translate the concept into a wearable silhouette. Every cap is drawn from multiple angles, and the yarn palette is chosen with the same care as a painter selects oils.

03

Step 03

Crochet & Craft

The hook meets the yarn. Each stitch builds texture, depth, and personality. This is where the magic happens — hours of focused, meditative handwork that no machine can replicate.

04

Step 04

Finishing & Detail

Embroidery, beadwork, custom tags — the finishing details that elevate a handcrafted piece into a statement. I inspect every inch under natural light.

05

Step 05

Final Product

The cap is steamed, photographed, and packed by hand. It ships with a story card detailing its inspiration — so the new owner knows exactly what they're wearing.
